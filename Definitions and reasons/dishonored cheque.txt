Dishonored cheques are recorded as a decrease from the cash at bank account. The cash discount that has previously been given will also be withdrawn.
Reasons why a cheque may be dishonored includes:
	Cheque has expired.
	Cheque is post-dated. (money will not be sent now, until the date that is stated on the cheque)
	Information on cheque is not consistent. (Amount written in numbers does not tally to the amount written in letters)
	Information on cheque is not complete. (There is no date, amount to be paid, recipient or signature)
	Payer's bank account is frozen, closed or does not have enough funds to carry out the transaction
